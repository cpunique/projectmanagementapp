The read

What the multi-panel test shows

A (flush) — the panels read as one continuous piece of app chrome. Dividers separate them cleanly, the board stays the focus, and adding a 4th costs nothing visually. Calm.

B (floating glass) — beautiful alone, but three floating cards jammed edge-to-edge create competing shadows and gaps. Each panel shouts "I'm a separate floating thing," and they fight each other. This is why the 4-panel screenshot read as busy.

C (flush + light glass) — flush behavior with a hint of depth. Board shows through faintly. Safe middle if A feels too flat.

Recommendation

A or C. Docked panels are chrome, not content — they should recede and let the board be the star. Glass + radius + big shadow is the modal language (Share, Help, dropdowns) — things that float over and demand attention. Analytics is the outlier that should change, not the reference the others copy.

Dev — the shared shell

Extract <PanelShell> owning: container (bg/border/shadow), the spring animation (stiffness:300, damping:30 — currently copy-pasted in all 4), header (title + subtitle + close), and the scroll body. Each panel supplies only its content. Fixes audit items 1–11 + 18 at once, and makes a 5th panel automatically consistent.

Header is now uniform: 16px padding, title 17px/600 var(--text), subtitle 11.5px var(--muted), close 28×28 var(--surface-2)/var(--border), divider var(--border). Due Dates gets a close button (currently has none).

Legitimate differences stay: Analytics keeps its stat tiles/charts/purple section headings; Due Dates keeps resizable width + semantic left-accent borders; Activity keeps divider-separated dense rows. The frame is identical; the content differs appropriately.
